# Stage 2: Customer Creation

> "Nail it before you scale it." — Steve Blank

## Purpose

Find **Business Model Fit** — Validate that:
1. You can acquire customers profitably
2. Unit economics work
3. Growth is repeatable

---

## Key Activities

### Optimize Unit Economics

- [ ] Calculate CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)
- [ ] Calculate LTV (Lifetime Value)
- [ ] Achieve LTV > 3x CAC
- [ ] Reduce time to payback

### Find Scalable Channels

- [ ] Test multiple acquisition channels
- [ ] Find channels that scale
- [ ] Document what works

### Build Growth Engine

- [ ] Identify growth levers
- [ ] Create repeatable playbooks
- [ ] Measure and optimize

---

## Key Metrics

| Metric | Formula | Current | Target |
|--------|---------|---------|--------|
| **CAC** | Total acquisition cost / New customers | $ | $ |
| **LTV** | Avg revenue × Avg lifetime | $ | $ |
| **LTV:CAC** | LTV / CAC | X:1 | >3:1 |
| **Payback Period** | CAC / Monthly revenue | X months | <12 months |

---

## Growth Engine Types

Which type are you building?

| Type | How It Works | Key Metric |
|------|--------------|------------|
| **Sticky** | High retention, low churn | Churn rate |
| **Viral** | Users invite users | Viral coefficient |
| **Paid** | Spend money to acquire | CAC vs LTV |

**Our primary engine:** [Sticky / Viral / Paid]

---

## Exit Criteria

_You're ready to move to Company Building when:_

| Criteria | Evidence |
|----------|----------|
| Unit economics positive | LTV > 3x CAC |
| Growth is repeatable | Consistent month-over-month |
| Channels identified | 2+ working channels |
| Playbooks documented | Anyone can execute |

---

## Common Mistakes

- ❌ Scaling with negative unit economics
- ❌ Relying on a single channel
- ❌ Growing faster than you can support
- ❌ Ignoring churn while focusing on acquisition
