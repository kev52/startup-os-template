# Stage 1: Customer Validation

> "A startup is a temporary organization designed to search for a repeatable and scalable business model." — Steve Blank

## Purpose

Find **Product/Market Fit** — Validate that:
1. People will use your product
2. People will pay for your product
3. You can deliver the product

---

## Key Activities

### Build MVP

- [ ] Build the minimum viable product
- [ ] Focus on core value proposition only
- [ ] Don't over-engineer

### Get Users

- [ ] Get early adopters to use the product
- [ ] Observe actual usage (not just sign-ups)
- [ ] Collect feedback and iterate

### Get Paying Customers

- [ ] Convert users to paying customers
- [ ] Validate pricing
- [ ] Understand buying process

---

## Product/Market Fit Signals

| Signal | Not Yet | Getting Close | PMF |
|--------|---------|---------------|-----|
| **Usage** | Low engagement | Growing engagement | Organic growth |
| **Retention** | Most churn | Some return | Most return |
| **Revenue** | No revenue | Some paying | Sustainable |
| **Referrals** | None | Some | Word of mouth |
| **Survey** | "Nice to have" | "Disappointed" | "Very disappointed if gone" |

## Exit Criteria

_You're ready to move to Customer Creation when:_

| Criteria | Evidence |
|----------|----------|
| Users love it | 40%+ would be "very disappointed" without it |
| Revenue | Paying customers exist |
| Retention | Users come back |
| Repeatable | You can acquire customers repeatedly |

---

## Common Mistakes

- ❌ Scaling before PMF
- ❌ Adding features instead of fixing core
- ❌ Ignoring churn
- ❌ Vanity metrics (sign-ups vs. active users)
